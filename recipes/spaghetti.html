Spaghetti

About Spaghetti:

Spaghetti is a type of pasta that is long, thin, and cylindrical, typically made of durum wheat flour.

It is a staple food in Italian cuisine and is often served with tomato sauce, meat, or vegetables.

It is typically cooked al dente, which means it is slightly firm to the bite.

It is commonly served with a variety of sauces, including marinara, pomodoro, and pesto.

Spaghetti can also be served with meat, such as meatballs or sausage, or with vegetables, such as broccoli or spinach.

List of ingredients:

  • 1 pound spaghetti noodles
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti noodles and cook according to package directions until al dente.
  3. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper to the skillet.
  6. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  7. Drain the pasta and add it to the skillet with the sauce.
  8. Toss to coat.
  9. Serve the spaghetti with grated Parmesan cheese.
